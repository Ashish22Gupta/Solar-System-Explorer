🪐 Jupiter – The Giant of the Solar System

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System. This gas giant is known for its incredible mass, iconic banded appearance, strong magnetic field, and the Great Red Spot – a gigantic storm raging for centuries.

Key Facts about Jupiter

  • Diameter: ~139,820 km (11.2× Earth's)
  • Mass: 1.9 × 10²⁷ kg (318× Earth)
  • Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years
  • Day Length: ~9.9 hours (fastest spinner)
  • Moons: 95+ (Ganymede, Europa, Io, Callisto...)
  • Magnetic Field: 20,000× stronger than Earth
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, ammonia storms
🌪 Interesting Fact: Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a vast storm over 1.3× Earth’s width. It’s been raging for over 350 years and is slowly shrinking — but remains extremely intense.

🚀 NASA Missions

Galileo (1995–2003): Explored Jupiter and its moons, atmosphere, and magnetic field.
Juno (2016–present): Currently in orbit, uncovering secrets of Jupiter’s interior and polar magnetosphere.

🌍 ESA & Global Research

JUICE Mission (2023–): ESA’s Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer is headed to study Ganymede, Callisto, and Europa in-depth.
Earth-based observatories and Hubble also track Jupiter's atmospheric changes and auroras.
