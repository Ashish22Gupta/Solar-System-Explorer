🪐 Pluto – The Dwarf Planet

Pluto, once the ninth planet of our Solar System, was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. Despite its small size and icy distance, Pluto captured the imagination of scientists and the public alike with its diverse surface and atmospheric mysteries.

Key Facts about Pluto

  • Diameter: ~2,377 km
  • Mass: ~0.0022× Earth
  • Distance from Sun: ~5.9 billion km
  • Orbital Period: 248 Earth years
  • Day Length: ~6.4 Earth days
  • Temperature: ~−229°C (−380°F)
  • Atmosphere: Thin; nitrogen, methane, CO
  • Moons: 5 (largest: Charon)
💜 Interesting Fact: Pluto’s surface has a heart-shaped nitrogen glacier called Tombaugh Regio. This dynamic icy region shocked scientists by showing geologic activity on what was assumed to be a frozen, inactive world.

🌐 NASA – New Horizons

The New Horizons spacecraft performed a historic flyby in 2015, delivering the first close-up views of Pluto’s icy mountains, haze-filled skies, and complex surface features.

🌐 IAU & Kuiper Belt Research

The International Astronomical Union reclassified Pluto as a dwarf planet in 2006. However, it remains crucial to Kuiper Belt studies — a region of icy bodies at the edge of our Solar System.

🔭 Ongoing Global Studies

Ground-based telescopes and Hubble continue to monitor Pluto’s thin atmosphere and surface changes. Plans for new missions to the outer Solar System and Kuiper Belt are under development.
