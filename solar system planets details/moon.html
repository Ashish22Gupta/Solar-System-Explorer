🌕 The Moon – Earth's Natural Satellite

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth-largest in the Solar System. It shapes tides, stabilizes Earth’s rotation, and has long inspired human culture, exploration, and science.

Key Facts about the Moon

  • Diameter: ~3,474 km (1/4 of Earth’s)
  • Mass: 7.35 × 10²² kg (1.2% of Earth’s mass)
  • Distance from Earth: ~384,400 km
  • Surface Gravity: 1/6th of Earth’s
  • Orbital Period: ~27.3 days
  • Atmosphere: Exosphere (very thin)
  • Temperature Range: -173°C to +127°C
  • Phases: New, First Quarter, Full, Last Quarter
🌒 Interesting Fact: The Moon is tidally locked — we always see the same side. The far side, once unseen, was first revealed by spacecraft and has a completely different terrain.

🌐 NASA (USA)

Apollo 11 was the first human landing in 1969. NASA’s Artemis program aims to return astronauts, including the first woman, to the Moon by the mid-2020s.

🌐 ISRO (India)

ISRO launched Chandrayaan-1 in 2008 (discovered water), and successfully landed Chandrayaan-3 near the Moon's south pole in 2023 — a global first.

🌐 CNSA (China)

Chang’e 4 landed on the far side of the Moon, and Chang’e 5 brought back lunar samples in 2020, advancing China’s lunar program.

🌐 JAXA (Japan)

SELENE (Kaguya) orbited and mapped the Moon in detail. Japan’s contributions help scientists study the Moon’s formation and internal structure.

🌐 Roscosmos (Russia)

Russia’s Luna 2 was the first spacecraft to reach the Moon in 1959. The Luna-25 mission and future plans aim to explore and establish a Moon base with international cooperation.
