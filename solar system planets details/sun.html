☀️ The Sun – Heart of Our Solar System

The Sun is a giant sphere of glowing gas at the center of our Solar System. Powered by nuclear fusion, it produces the light and energy necessary to sustain life on Earth and governs the motion of all planetary bodies in its gravitational domain.

Key Facts about the Sun

  • Diameter: ~1.39 million km (109× Earth)
  • Mass: ~1.989 × 10²⁹ kg (330,000× Earth)
  • Composition: 73% hydrogen, 25% helium
  • Core Temp: ~15 million °C (27 million °F)
  • Surface Temp: ~5,500 °C (9,932 °F)
  • Distance from Earth: ~150 million km
  • Age: ~4.6 billion years
  • Expected Lifespan: ~10 billion years
🔥 Interesting Fact: NASA’s Parker Solar Probe is the first spacecraft to “touch” the Sun by entering its outer atmosphere (the corona), gathering crucial data on solar wind and space weather.

🌐 NASA (USA)

NASA leads solar exploration with missions like Parker Solar Probe and Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO), studying solar activity and helping forecast space weather that affects satellites and power grids.

🌐 ESA (Europe)

The Solar Orbiter, a joint ESA–NASA mission, provides close-up images of the Sun’s poles and improves our understanding of solar cycles and the heliosphere.

🌐 ISRO (India)

India's Aditya-L1, launched in 2023, is its first solar mission. It monitors the solar corona and flares from Lagrange Point L1 to enhance space weather prediction models.

🌐 JAXA (Japan)

Hinode (Solar-B), developed with ESA, explores the Sun's magnetic field and solar flares, helping scientists understand mechanisms behind space weather events.

🌐 CNSA (China)

China’s ASO-S mission (2022) studies solar flares and CMEs (Coronal Mass Ejections), enhancing global understanding of solar radiation and particle bursts.

🌐 Roscosmos (Russia)

Russia has supported solar science with legacy observatories and ongoing international partnerships. New projects aim to study the Sun’s influence on Earth’s climate and magnetosphere.
