🌋 Venus – Earth's Twin Planet

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth’s twin due to its similar size and composition. However, its extremely dense atmosphere traps heat through a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in the Solar System.

Key Facts about Venus

  • Diameter: ~12,104 km (95% of Earth)
  • Mass: 4.87 × 10²⁴ kg (81.5% of Earth)
  • Atmosphere: 96.5% CO₂ with sulfuric acid clouds
  • Surface Pressure: 92× Earth’s
  • Temperature: ~465°C (869°F)
  • Day Length: 243 Earth days (retrograde)
  • Year Length: 225 Earth days
  • Moons: None
  • Magnetic Field: Extremely weak
🔥 Interesting Fact: A day on Venus is longer than its year! It also spins in the opposite direction compared to most planets — a rare retrograde rotation.

🌐 NASA (USA)

Magellan mapped 98% of Venus’s surface using radar (1989–1994). Upcoming missions like VERITAS and DAVINCI+ aim to explore the planet’s geology and atmosphere in greater detail.

🌐 ESA (Europe)

Venus Express orbited Venus from 2005–2014 and studied its dense atmosphere and volcanic surface features.

🌐 ISRO (India)

ISRO is developing Shukrayaan-1, its first mission to Venus, aiming to study atmospheric chemistry and surface interaction.

🌐 Roscosmos (Russia)

The Venera missions of the Soviet era achieved the first landings on Venus. Venera 7 was the first spacecraft to transmit data from the surface of another planet.

🌐 JAXA (Japan)

Akatsuki entered Venus’s orbit in 2015 and continues to study its dynamic weather patterns, clouds, and mysterious super-rotation of the upper atmosphere.
