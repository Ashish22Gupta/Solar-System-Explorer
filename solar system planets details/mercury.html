☿ Mercury – The Swift Planet

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It zips around the Sun in just 88 Earth days, making it the fastest planet in the Solar System. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury isn't the hottest—Venus holds that record.

Key Facts about Mercury

  • Diameter: ~4,879 km (38% of Earth's)
  • Mass: 0.055× Earth’s mass
  • Distance from Sun: ~58 million km (0.39 AU)
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Day Length: 176 Earth days
  • Temperature Range: -173°C to 427°C
  • Atmosphere: Exosphere — extremely thin
  • Moons: None
🧲 Interesting Fact: Mercury’s iron-rich core takes up 85% of its radius. This gives it a magnetic field, rare for such a small planet. Water ice has been detected in permanently shadowed polar craters!

🚀 NASA – MESSENGER Mission

NASA’s MESSENGER orbited Mercury from 2011–2015. It mapped the entire surface, discovered ancient volcanic activity, and confirmed the presence of water ice and organic material in deep craters.

🌍 ESA & JAXA – BepiColombo

The BepiColombo mission, a collaboration between ESA and JAXA, launched in 2018 and is set to arrive in 2025. It will study Mercury’s magnetic field, surface geology, and thin atmosphere in detail.

🔬 Earth-Based Observations

Observatories on Earth use radar and telescopes to track Mercury’s orbit, rotation, and surface temperatures. These findings help scientists understand how rocky planets evolve close to their stars.
