🌀 Uranus – The Tilted Ice Giant

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third-largest by diameter. With an extreme axial tilt of 98°, it essentially spins on its side — a unique trait likely caused by a massive ancient collision. Uranus is an icy gas giant with a pale blue atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Key Facts about Uranus

  • Diameter: ~50,724 km
  • Mass: 8.68 × 10²⁵ kg (14.5× Earth)
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane
  • Temperature: ~−224°C (coldest planet)
  • Moons: 27 (e.g., Titania, Oberon)
  • Rings: 13 faint rings
  • Day Length: ~17 hours
  • Year Length: ~84 Earth years
  • Axial Tilt: 98°
🧊 Interesting Fact: Uranus is the only planet that rotates on its side — its axis is almost parallel to the plane of the Solar System, likely the result of a massive impact billions of years ago.

🌐 NASA (USA)

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited Uranus, flying by in 1986. It revealed 11 new moons, detected a magnetic field, and observed the planet’s rings and tilted axis. A Uranus Orbiter & Probe mission is under development for the 2030s.

🌐 ESA (Europe)

ESA is exploring potential collaboration with NASA on future Uranus missions focused on studying its atmosphere, magnetosphere, and icy moons.

🔭 Research Highlights

Observations by the Hubble Space Telescope and Keck Observatory continue to track Uranus's evolving storm systems and seasonal changes over its long orbital period.
