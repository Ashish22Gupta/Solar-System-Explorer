🌍 Earth - The Blue Planet

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to harbor life. With its vast oceans, dynamic atmosphere, and protective magnetic field, Earth is our unique home in the universe.

Key Facts about Earth

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Mass: 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg
  • Surface Area: ~510 million km²
  • Orbit: 365.26 days per year
  • Rotation: ~24 hours
  • Atmosphere: 78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen
  • Water Coverage: 71% of surface
  • Moons: 1 (The Moon)
  • Magnetic Field: Generated by molten iron core
🌟 Did you know? Earth’s magnetic field not only protects us from solar radiation but also helps animals like birds and turtles navigate during migration.

🌐 NASA (USA)

NASA’s Earth Observing System (EOS) includes Aqua, Terra, and Suomi NPP satellites that monitor Earth’s climate, atmosphere, and surface. These missions aid global weather and climate research.

🌐 ESA (European Space Agency)

The Copernicus Programme and Sentinel satellites track air quality, deforestation, sea-level rise, and urban development — contributing to one of the largest Earth observation systems.

🌐 ISRO (India)

ISRO’s INSAT, Oceansat, and EOS satellites help with climate monitoring, disaster management, and agricultural planning. EOS-06 focuses on ocean climate and marine ecosystems.

🌐 JAXA (Japan)

JAXA’s GOSAT and ALOS programs observe greenhouse gases and monitor natural disasters. These satellites assist in climate change studies and emergency response.

🌐 CNSA (China)

China’s Fengyun and Gaofen satellites provide vital data for weather forecasting, environmental monitoring, and agricultural development through the CHEOS initiative.

🌐 Roscosmos (Russia)

Russia’s Meteor-M satellites monitor climate systems and ice coverage, particularly in polar regions. They support global research on seasonal changes and natural hazards.
